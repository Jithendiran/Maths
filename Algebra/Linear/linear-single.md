**$$\mathbf{\frac{w}{-2} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{2}{3}}$$**

*Method 1*
----

Remove $\frac{1}{6}$ by doing inverse $-\frac{1}{6}$ on both side

$$\frac{w}{-2} + \cancel{\frac{1}{6}} \cancel{-\frac{1}{6}} = \frac{2}{3} -\frac{1}{6}$$

Now remove $\frac{}{-2}$ by doind inverse $\times -2$

$$\frac{\cancel{-2} }{1}\times \frac{w}{\cancel{-2}}  = \frac{-2}{1} \times \frac{2}{3} -  \frac{-2}{1} \times \frac{1}{6}$$

in right side of the equation move  $\frac{-2}{1}$ out as common

$$w = \frac{-2}{1}(\frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{6})$$

LCM (2, 3, 6) = 6

$$w = \frac{-2}{1}(\frac{2 \times 2}{3 \times 2} - \frac{1 \times 1}{6 \times 1}) =  \frac{-2}{1}(\frac{4}{6} - \frac{1}{6})$$

$$w = \frac{-2}{1}(\frac{\cancel{3}^1}{\cancel{6}^2}) = \frac{\cancel{-2}}{1} \times \frac{1}{\cancel{2}}$$

$$w = -1$$

*method 2*
---

Remove $\frac{1}{6}$ by doing inverse $-\frac{1}{6}$ on both side

$$\frac{w}{-2} + \cancel{\frac{1}{6}} \cancel{-\frac{1}{6}} = \frac{2}{3} -\frac{1}{6}$$

Convert the fraction to same unit LCM (3, 6)= 6

$$\frac{-w \times 3}{-2 \times 3} = \frac{2 \times 2}{3 \times 2} - \frac{1 \times 1}{6 \times 1}$$

$$\frac{-3w}{6} = \frac{4}{6} - \frac{1}{6}$$

Remove $\frac{}{6}$ by inverse 

$$\frac{\cancel{6}}{1} * \frac{-3w}{\cancel{6}} = (\frac{\cancel{6}}{1} * \frac{4}{\cancel{6}}) - ( \frac{\cancel{6}}{1} * \frac{1}{\cancel{6}})$$

$$-3w = 3$$

remove -3 from w, -3 is multiplied to w so we have to divide as per iversion

$$\frac{\cancel{-3}w}{\cancel{-3}} = \frac{\cancel{3}}{-\cancel{3}}$$

$$w = -1$$

## variable on both sides

$$3x + 7 = 5x - 1$$

1. **Collect the Variable Terms**

We will move the smaller $x$-term ($3x$) to the side of the larger $x$-term ($5x$) to keep the coefficient of $x$ positive.

$$3x + 7 = 5x - 1$$
To move $3x$
$$\cancel{3x}\cancel{-3x}+7 = 5x-3x-1$$

simplify this

$$7 = 2x-1$$


2. **Collect the Constant Terms**

We will move the constant term $(-1)$ from the right side (RHS) to the left side (LHS).

add both side by $+1$
$$7 + 1= 2x \cancel{- 1 }\cancel{+ 1}$$


3. **Isolate the Variable**

The term $2x$ means $2$ is multiplied by $x$. We must eliminate the coefficient $2$.

$$8 = 2x$$

Divide both side by $2x$

$$\frac{\cancel{8}4}{\cancel{2}1} = \frac{\cancel{2}x}{\cancel{2}}$$

$$\mathbf{x = 4}$$

**Verification**

Substitute $x = 4$ back into the original equation:

$$3x + 7 = 5x - 1$$
$$3(4) + 7 = 5(4) - 1$$
$$12 + 7 = 20 - 1$$
$$19 = 19$$


## more alike term

$$9 - 4y + 11y = 2y - 1 + 15$$

1. **Simplify Both Sides (Combine Like Terms)**

First, combine the $y$-terms and the constant terms on each side of the equation.

**Left-Hand Side (LHS):**
$$9 \mathbf{- 4y + 11y} = 9 + 7y$$

**Right-Hand Side (RHS):**
$$\mathbf{2y} \mathbf{- 1 + 15} = 2y + 14$$

**New Equation:**
$$\mathbf{9 + 7y = 2y + 14}$$

2. **Collect the Variable Terms**

$$9 + 7y = 2y + 14$$

We move the smaller $y$-term ($2y$) to the side of the larger $y$-term ($7y$). We eliminate $2y$ from the RHS by subtracting $2y$ from both sides.

$$9 + 7y - 2y = \cancel{2y}\cancel{-2y} + 14$$

**Simplify:**

$$\mathbf{9 + 5y = 14}$$

3. **Collect the Constant Terms**

$$9 + 5y = 14$$

We move the constant term ($9$) from the LHS to the RHS. We eliminate $9$ by subtracting $9$ from both sides.

$$\cancel{9}\cancel{-9} + 5y = 14 - 9$$

**Simplify:**

$$\mathbf{5y = 5}$$

4. **Isolate the Variable**

$$5y = 5$$

The term $5y$ means $5$ is multiplied by $y$. We eliminate the coefficient $5$ by dividing both sides by $5$.

$$\frac{\cancel{5}y}{\cancel{5}} = \frac{5}{5}$$

**Simplify:**

$$\mathbf{y = 1}$$

**Verification**

Substitute $\mathbf{y = 1}$ back into the original equation:

$$9 - 4y + 11y = 2y - 1 + 15$$
$$9 - 4(1) + 11(1) = 2(1) - 1 + 15$$
$$9 - 4 + 11 = 2 - 1 + 15$$
$$5 + 11 = 1 + 15$$
$$\mathbf{16 = 16}$$

The solution is verified.

$$\mathbf{0.2z + 5 - z + 1.5 = 10 - 4z + 0.3}$$

*method1*
---

1. **Simplify Both Sides (Combine Like Terms)**

First, combine the $z$-terms and the constant terms on each side of the equation.

**Left-Hand Side (LHS):**
$$(0.2z - z) + (5 + 1.5) = -0.8z + 6.5$$

**Right-Hand Side (RHS):**
$$- 4z + (10 + 0.3) = -4z + 10.3$$

**New Equation:**
$$-0.8z + 6.5 = -4z + 10.3$$

2. **Collect the Variable Terms**

We move the smaller $z$-term (which is $-4z$) to the side of the larger $z$-term (which is $-0.8z$). We eliminate $-4z$ from the RHS by **adding** $4z$ to both sides.

$$-0.8z + 6.5 = -4z + 10.3$$

$$-0.8z + 4z + 6.5 = \cancel{-4z}\cancel{+4z} + 10.3$$

**Simplify:**
$$3.2z + 6.5 = 10.3$$

3. **Collect the Constant Terms**

We move the constant term ($6.5$) from the LHS to the RHS. We eliminate $6.5$ by **subtracting** $6.5$ from both sides.

$$3.2z + 6.5 = 10.3$$

$$3.2z \cancel{+ 6.5}\cancel{- 6.5} = 10.3 - 6.5$$

**Simplify:**
$$3.2z = 3.8$$

4. **Isolate the Variable**

The term $3.2z$ means $3.2$ is multiplied by $z$. We eliminate the coefficient $3.2$ by **dividing** both sides by $3.2$.

$$3.2z = 3.8$$

$$\frac{\cancel{3.2}z}{\cancel{3.2}} = \frac{3.8}{3.2}$$

**Simplify (Convert to fraction or decimal):**

$$z = \frac{3.8}{3.2} = \frac{38}{32}$$

$$\mathbf{z = \frac{19}{16} \text{ or } z = 1.1875}$$

*method 2*
---

1. **Simplify Both Sides (Combine Like Terms)**

Combine the $z$-terms and the constant terms on each side.

**Left-Hand Side (LHS):**
$$(0.2z - z) + (5 + 1.5) = -0.8z + 6.5$$

**Right-Hand Side (RHS):**
$$- 4z + (10 + 0.3) = -4z + 10.3$$

**Simplified Equation:**
$$-0.8z + 6.5 = -4z + 10.3$$

2. **Convert Decimals to Integers**

The largest number of decimal places in any term is one. To eliminate all decimals, we multiply **every term** on both sides of the equation by $10$.

$$10 \times (-0.8z + 6.5) = 10 \times (-4z + 10.3)$$

$$-8z + 65 = -40z + 103$$

**New Integer Equation:**
$$-8z + 65 = -40z + 103$$

3. **Collect the Variable Terms**

We move the smaller $z$-term (which is $-40z$) to the side of the larger $z$-term (which is $-8z$). We eliminate $-40z$ from the RHS by **adding** $40z$ to both sides.

$$-8z + 65 = -40z + 103$$

$$-8z + 40z + 65 = \cancel{-40z}\cancel{+40z} + 103$$

**Simplify:**
$$32z + 65 = 103$$

4. **Collect the Constant Terms**

We move the constant term ($65$) from the LHS to the RHS. We eliminate $65$ by **subtracting** $65$ from both sides.

$$32z + 65 = 103$$

$$32z \cancel{+ 65}\cancel{- 65} = 103 - 65$$

**Simplify:**
$$32z = 38$$

We eliminate the coefficient $32$ by **dividing** both sides by $32$.

$$32z = 38$$

$$\mathbf{4.5k - 7 + 1.05k = 12.0005 - 2k + 0.5}$$

*method 1*
---

**Simplify Both Sides (Combine Like Terms)**

Combine the $k$-terms and the constant terms on each side of the equation.

**Left-Hand Side (LHS):**
$$(4.5k + 1.05k) - 7 = 5.55k - 7$$

**Right-Hand Side (RHS):**
$$- 2k + (12.0005 + 0.5) = -2k + 12.5005$$

**New Equation:**
$$5.55k - 7 = -2k + 12.5005$$


**Collect the Variable Terms**

We move the smaller $k$-term (which is $-2k$) to the side of the larger $k$-term ($5.55k$). Eliminate $-2k$ from the RHS by **adding** $2k$ to both sides.

$$5.55k + 2k - 7 = \cancel{-2k}\cancel{+2k} + 12.5005$$

$$7.55k - 7 = 12.5005$$


**Collect the Constant Terms**

We move the constant term ($-7$) from the LHS to the RHS. Eliminate $-7$ by **adding** $7$ to both sides.

$$7.55k \cancel{- 7}\cancel{+ 7} = 12.5005 + 7$$

$$7.55k = 19.5005$$


**Isolate the Variable**

We eliminate the coefficient $7.55$ by **dividing** both sides by $7.55$.

$$k = \frac{19.5005}{7.55}$$

**Simplify (Resulting Value):**
$$k \approx 2.58$$

*method 2*
---

**Determine the Multiplier**
Identify the smallest power of 10 that will eliminate all decimal places in the equation:
$$4.5k - 7 + 1.05k = 12.0005 - 2k + 0.5$$

The decimal terms are $4.5$ (1 decimal place), $1.05$ (2 decimal places), $12.0005$ (4 decimal places), and $0.5$ (1 decimal place).

The **maximum number of decimal places is 4** (from $12.0005$). Therefore, you must **multiply the entire equation by $10^4$, or $10,000$**.

**Multiply the Entire Equation by $10,000$**
Multiply every single term on both sides by $10,000$:

$$10,000 \times (4.5k - 7 + 1.05k) = 10,000 \times (12.0005 - 2k + 0.5)$$

$$(10000 \times 4.5k) - (10000 \times 7) + (10000 \times 1.05k)  = (10000 \times 12.0005) - (10000 \times 2k) + (10000 \times 0.5) $$

*method 1*
---

**Convert float to decimal**

* $\frac{1}{3} \approx 0.33$

* $\frac{5}{6} \approx 0.83$
* $1\frac{1}{2} = 1.5$
* $0.75 = 0.75$

$$0.33x + 2.5 - 5 = 0.83x + 1.5 - 0.75x$$

**Simplify Both Sides (Combine Like Terms)**


**Left-Hand Side (LHS):**
Combine constant terms: $2.5 - 5 = -2.5$
$$0.33x + (2.5 - 5) = 0.33x - 2.5$$

**Right-Hand Side (RHS):**
Combine $x$-terms: $0.83x - 0.75x = 0.08x$
$$(0.83x - 0.75x) + 1.5 = 0.08x + 1.5$$

**New Equation (using approximations):**

$$0.33x - 2.5 = 0.08x+1.5$$

**Move constant** 

$$0.33x \cancel{-2.5 + 2.5} = 0.08x + 1.5 + 2.5$$

Simplify

$$0.33x = 0.08x + 4$$

**Move variable**

$$0.33x -0.08x= \cancel{0.08x} + 4 \cancel{-0.08x}$$


$$0.33x - 0.08x = 4 $$

**Convert to Int**

0.33 has 2 digit and 0.08 has 2 digit so multiply by 100

$$100 \times (0.33) - 100 \times (0.08x) = 100 \times (4) $$
The new equation with only integers is: 
$$33x - 8x = 400$$
$$25x = 400$$

$$x = \frac{400}{25} = 16$$
