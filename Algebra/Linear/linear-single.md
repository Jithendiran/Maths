**$$\mathbf{\frac{w}{-2} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{2}{3}}$$**

*Method 1*
----

Remove $\frac{1}{6}$ by doing inverse $-\frac{1}{6}$ on both side

$$\frac{w}{-2} + \cancel{\frac{1}{6}} \cancel{-\frac{1}{6}} = \frac{2}{3} -\frac{1}{6}$$

Now remove $\frac{}{-2}$ by doind inverse $\times -2$

$$\frac{\cancel{-2} }{1}\times \frac{w}{\cancel{-2}}  = \frac{-2}{1} \times \frac{2}{3} -  \frac{-2}{1} \times \frac{1}{6}$$

in right side of the equation move  $\frac{-2}{1}$ out as common

$$w = \frac{-2}{1}(\frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{6})$$

Clearing the fraction, LCM (2, 3, 6) = 6

$$w = \frac{-2}{1}(\frac{2 \times 2}{3 \times 2} - \frac{1 \times 1}{6 \times 1}) =  \frac{-2}{1}(\frac{4}{6} - \frac{1}{6})$$

$$w = \frac{-2}{1}(\frac{\cancel{3}^1}{\cancel{6}^2}) = \frac{\cancel{-2}}{1} \times \frac{1}{\cancel{2}}$$

$$w = -1$$

## variable on both sides

$$3x + 7 = 5x - 1$$

1. **Collect the Variable Terms**

We will move the smaller $x$-term ($3x$) to the side of the larger $x$-term ($5x$) to keep the coefficient of $x$ positive.

$$3x + 7 = 5x - 1$$
To move $3x$
$$\cancel{3x}\cancel{-3x}+7 = 5x-3x-1$$

simplify this

$$7 = 2x-1$$


2. **Collect the Constant Terms**

We will move the constant term $(-1)$ from the right side (RHS) to the left side (LHS).

add both side by $+1$
$$7 + 1= 2x \cancel{- 1 }\cancel{+ 1}$$


3. **Isolate the Variable**

The term $2x$ means $2$ is multiplied by $x$. We must eliminate the coefficient $2$.

$$8 = 2x$$

Divide both side by $2x$

$$\frac{\cancel{8}4}{\cancel{2}1} = \frac{\cancel{2}x}{\cancel{2}}$$

$$\mathbf{x = 4}$$

**Verification**

Substitute $x = 4$ back into the original equation:

$$3x + 7 = 5x - 1$$
$$3(4) + 7 = 5(4) - 1$$
$$12 + 7 = 20 - 1$$
$$19 = 19$$


## more alike term

$$9 - 4y + 11y = 2y - 1 + 15$$

1. **Simplify Both Sides (Combine Like Terms)**

First, combine the $y$-terms and the constant terms on each side of the equation.

**Left-Hand Side (LHS):**
$$9 \mathbf{- 4y + 11y} = 9 + 7y$$

**Right-Hand Side (RHS):**
$$\mathbf{2y} \mathbf{- 1 + 15} = 2y + 14$$

**New Equation:**
$$\mathbf{9 + 7y = 2y + 14}$$

2. **Collect the Variable Terms**

$$9 + 7y = 2y + 14$$

We move the smaller $y$-term ($2y$) to the side of the larger $y$-term ($7y$). We eliminate $2y$ from the RHS by subtracting $2y$ from both sides.

$$9 + 7y - 2y = \cancel{2y}\cancel{-2y} + 14$$

**Simplify:**

$$\mathbf{9 + 5y = 14}$$

3. **Collect the Constant Terms**

$$9 + 5y = 14$$

We move the constant term ($9$) from the LHS to the RHS. We eliminate $9$ by subtracting $9$ from both sides.

$$\cancel{9}\cancel{-9} + 5y = 14 - 9$$

**Simplify:**

$$\mathbf{5y = 5}$$

4. **Isolate the Variable**

$$5y = 5$$

The term $5y$ means $5$ is multiplied by $y$. We eliminate the coefficient $5$ by dividing both sides by $5$.

$$\frac{\cancel{5}y}{\cancel{5}} = \frac{5}{5}$$

**Simplify:**

$$\mathbf{y = 1}$$

**Verification**

Substitute $\mathbf{y = 1}$ back into the original equation:

$$9 - 4y + 11y = 2y - 1 + 15$$
$$9 - 4(1) + 11(1) = 2(1) - 1 + 15$$
$$9 - 4 + 11 = 2 - 1 + 15$$
$$5 + 11 = 1 + 15$$
$$\mathbf{16 = 16}$$

The solution is verified.

$$\mathbf{0.2z + 5 - z + 1.5 = 10 - 4z + 0.3}$$

*method1*
---

1. **Simplify Both Sides (Combine Like Terms)**

First, combine the $z$-terms and the constant terms on each side of the equation.

**Left-Hand Side (LHS):**
$$(0.2z - z) + (5 + 1.5) = -0.8z + 6.5$$

**Right-Hand Side (RHS):**
$$- 4z + (10 + 0.3) = -4z + 10.3$$

**New Equation:**
$$-0.8z + 6.5 = -4z + 10.3$$

2. **Collect the Variable Terms**

We move the smaller $z$-term (which is $-4z$) to the side of the larger $z$-term (which is $-0.8z$). We eliminate $-4z$ from the RHS by **adding** $4z$ to both sides.

$$-0.8z + 6.5 = -4z + 10.3$$

$$-0.8z + 4z + 6.5 = \cancel{-4z}\cancel{+4z} + 10.3$$

**Simplify:**
$$3.2z + 6.5 = 10.3$$

3. **Collect the Constant Terms**

We move the constant term ($6.5$) from the LHS to the RHS. We eliminate $6.5$ by **subtracting** $6.5$ from both sides.

$$3.2z + 6.5 = 10.3$$

$$3.2z \cancel{+ 6.5}\cancel{- 6.5} = 10.3 - 6.5$$

**Simplify:**
$$3.2z = 3.8$$

4. **Isolate the Variable**

The term $3.2z$ means $3.2$ is multiplied by $z$. We eliminate the coefficient $3.2$ by **dividing** both sides by $3.2$.

$$3.2z = 3.8$$

$$\frac{\cancel{3.2}z}{\cancel{3.2}} = \frac{3.8}{3.2}$$

**Simplify (Convert to fraction or decimal):**

$$z = \frac{3.8}{3.2} = \frac{38}{32}$$

$$\mathbf{z = \frac{19}{16} \text{ or } z = 1.1875}$$

*method 1*
---

**Simplify Both Sides (Combine Like Terms)**

Combine the $k$-terms and the constant terms on each side of the equation.

**Left-Hand Side (LHS):**

**Right-Hand Side (RHS):**
$$- 2k + (12.0005 + 0.5) = -2k + 12.5005$$

$$5.55k - 7 = -2k + 12.5005$$

We move the smaller $k$-term (which is $-2k$) to the side of the larger $k$-term ($5.55k$). Eliminate $-2k$ from the RHS by **adding** $2k$ to both sides.

$$5.55k + 2k - 7 = \cancel{-2k}\cancel{+2k} + 12.5005$$

*method 2*
----

The original equation is:
$$\mathbf{\frac{3}{4}w - 1.25 + 7 = 2.5w + \frac{1}{5} - 0.003w}$$

**Convert All Terms to Fractions**

* $1.25 = 1 \frac{1}{4} = \frac{5}{4}$
* $7 = \frac{7}{1}$
* $2.5 = 2 \frac{1}{2} = \frac{5}{2}$
* $0.003 = \frac{3}{1000}$

**Equation in Fraction Form:**
$$\frac{3}{4}w - \frac{5}{4} + \frac{7}{1} = \frac{5}{2}w + \frac{1}{5} - \frac{3}{1000}w$$

**Simplify Both Sides (Combine Like Terms)**

Combine the constant terms on the LHS and the $w$-terms on the RHS.

* **LHS (Constants):** $-\frac{5}{4} + \frac{7}{1} = -\frac{5}{4} + \frac{28}{4} = \frac{23}{4}$
* **RHS ($w$-terms):** $\frac{5}{2}w - \frac{3}{1000}w$. LCM of $2$ and $1000$ is $1000$.
    $$\frac{5}{2}w - \frac{3}{1000}w = \frac{5 \cdot 500}{2 \cdot 500}w - \frac{3}{1000}w = \frac{2500}{1000}w - \frac{3}{1000}w = \frac{2497}{1000}w$$

**Simplified Equation:**
$$\frac{3}{4}w + \frac{23}{4} = \frac{2497}{1000}w + \frac{1}{5}$$

**Clear Fractions by Multiplying by the LCM**

The denominators are $4, 4, 1000, 5$.
The **LCM** of $(4, 1000, 5)$ is $1000$.

Multiply every term by $1000$:
$$1000 \cdot \left(\frac{3}{4}w\right) + 1000 \cdot \left(\frac{23}{4}\right) = 1000 \cdot \left(\frac{2497}{1000}w\right) + 1000 \cdot \left(\frac{1}{5}\right)$$

**Simplify the terms:**

**method2**

Simplified LHS and Unsimplified RHS

**LHS (from previous steps, already simplified):**
$$\text{LHS} = \frac{11x - 30}{36}$$

**RHS (Multiplying without cancelling $\mathbf{(x+2)}$):**

$$\text{RHS} = \frac{1}{5} \left( \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{x+2} \right) \left( \frac{10x+20}{3} \right)$$

Substitute the first term with its common denominator $\frac{x+4}{2(x+2)}$ and the second term with its factored form $\frac{10(x+2)}{3}$:

$$\text{RHS} = \frac{1}{5} \left( \frac{x+4}{2(x+2)} \right) \left( \frac{10(x+2)}{3} \right)$$

Now, multiply the numerators and denominators:

$$\text{RHS} = \frac{1 \cdot (x+4) \cdot 10(x+2)}{5 \cdot 2(x+2) \cdot 3} = \frac{10(x+4)(x+2)}{30(x+2)}$$


The resulting equation is:

$$\frac{11x - 30}{36} = \frac{10(x+4)(x+2)}{30(x+2)}$$

To clear the denominators, we can multiply both sides by $\text{LCM}[36, 30(x+2)] = 180(x+2)$.

$$180(x+2) \cdot \frac{11x - 30}{36} = 180(x+2) \cdot \frac{10(x+4)(x+2)}{30(x+2)}$$

$$5(x+2)(11x - 30) = 6 \cdot 10(x+4)(x+2)$$

$$5(11x^2 - 30x + 22x - 60) = 60(x^2 + 2x + 4x + 8)$$

$$5(11x^2 - 8x - 60) = 60(x^2 + 6x + 8)$$

$$55x^2 - 40x - 300 = 60x^2 + 360x + 480$$

Move all terms to one side to set the quadratic equation to zero:

$$(60x^2 - 55x^2) + (360x - (-40x)) + (480 - (-300)) = 0$$

$$5x^2 + 400x + 780 = 0$$

Divide by 5 to simplify:

$$x^2 + 80x + 156 = 0$$

Now, solve the quadratic equation (by factoring or the quadratic formula). Look for two numbers that multiply to $156$ and add up to $80$. The numbers are $78$ and $2$.

$$(x + 78)(x + 2) = 0$$

This gives two potential solutions for $x$:

1.  $$x + 78 = 0 \quad \rightarrow \quad \mathbf{x = -78}$$
2.  $$x + 2 = 0 \quad \rightarrow \quad \mathbf{x = -2}$$

In the original equation, the term $\frac{1}{x+2}$ means the equation is **undefined** when the denominator is zero, so $x \neq -2$.

Therefore, the only valid solution is:$$\mathbf{x = -78}$$
